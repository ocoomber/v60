Tasting your cup — what to feel for

Most brewing fixes start with naming the taste. Here's what each one feels like, and which way to nudge your grind.

Sour

Sharp and tart — like lemon or unripe fruit — and dries the sides of your tongue. Usually under-extracted: grind finer, use hotter water, or brew a little longer.

Bitter

Acrid, like ash or burnt toast, with a lingering dryness at the back of your tongue. Usually over-extracted: grind coarser, cooler water, or brew shorter.

Weak

Thin and watery, little flavour or body. Too little coffee or under-extracted: add dose or grind finer.

Astringent

A puckering, drying sensation, like over-steeped tea. Often too fine a grind or over-agitation: grind a touch coarser and pour more gently.

Dialling it in

Grind: if the brew finishes too fast and tastes weak/sour, grind finer. Too slow and bitter, grind coarser.

Dose: pick a method, then set how much coffee you want to spend — water and every pour scale to it.

Custom: each pour is a cumulative target. Make pour 1 smaller than pour 2 for a sweeter cup, or the reverse for more brightness.
